3-30-300 Summit: Shaping Tulsa's Urban Canopy
The Centennial Center in Veterans Park
🦩🦩🦩🦩🦩Gay-friendly
The 3-30-300 Summit is a free public conversation at the Centennial Center in Veterans Park about urban tree canopy: three trees per home, 30% canopy cover per neighborhood, 300 meters to the nearest park. These are the standards that separate livable cities from heat islands, and Tulsa is not meeting them equally across neighborhoods. Environmental justice and LGBTQ+ justice have always been connected -- the communities with least access to green space are disproportionately ours. This is the kind of civic engagement event where showing up shapes what actually happens. Thursday at 8 AM. Free. Come for the urban planning. Stay because you want to live in a better city.

Queen Bess Centennial Aviation Arts Festival: 'The Flying Ace' Silent Film at Circle Cinema. Bessie Coleman was the first Black woman and first Native American to earn a pilot's license. She did it in 1921, in France, because no American flight school would accept her. Tulsa is honoring her centennial with a free silent film and live musical performance.
